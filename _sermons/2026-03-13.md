If you are anything like me, you have a few things that you want to do that are
hard to do.  Sometimes they seem necessary but unpleasant.  Other times, they
just seem implausibly hard.  Or it’s just a matter of never having time because
of all the other things you’re doing.
 
You have some control over your calendar.  The thing needs to be done.  So make
the time, and at least get started.  Just do the thing.
 
There are many things in this world that would ideally be different, and that
you’re probably not in any position to do anything about.  And then there are
things within your control.  Worrying about the former can be a road to
depressed paralysis, and can keep you from making progress on the latter.  In a
time of war and upheaval, what does this thing that I’m trying to do matter?
 
It does matter.  So do the thing.
 
We get distracted.  We wait for inspiration.  And the things that we want to
get done are still there, and will probably remain there forever if we wait for
the perfect moment of inspiration and clarity when the world is at peace.  Your
first attempts will almost surely be imperfect.  That’s fine – it’s still
forward progress.
 
So get started on the thing.
 
We start off without all the skills we need.  There are barriers all over.  But
you can often move barriers if you work on them.  And if you don’t have all the
skills you need, well, we are all works in progress – you can learn.
 
So do the thing.
