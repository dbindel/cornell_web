A first-year graduate student in the US is often right around 22-23 years old.
This is somewhere between a third and a quarter of the way through a typical
lifespan, depending on how unlucky or lucky one might be.  It’s a smaller
fraction if we think of an “adult lifespan.”  Joe Halpern arrived at Cornell in
1996, before almost all of you were born.  You were all alive when I arrived in
2009, but many of you were probably young enough not to remember that year
particularly clearly.  It doesn’t feel that long ago to me.
 
When you have kids, you’ll inevitably have someone tell you that “the days are
long, but the years go fast.”  Kids make this more obvious, but it’s true
generally.  A semester feels like an enormous amount of time, and then it goes
by in what seems like the blink of an eye.  A PhD is a several year commitment,
and sometimes during the process it feels like an eternity – and at the same
time it goes surprisingly fast.  So when it feels like you’re struggling
forever, know that this too will pass; and when time seems to be flying by,
take a moment to appreciate what is good about the moment you are in.
