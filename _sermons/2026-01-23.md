Aspiration is a fundamental part of the identity of a university.  We want to
know; we want to grow; and we want to bring our students and colleagues (and
eventually the world) along for the ride.
 
By definition, aspiration is not about where we are.  It is about where we want
to be.  To get from where we are to where we want to be requires action and
discipline.  Ideally, we can support each other in reaching our aspirations,
and that is part of the university mission as well.
 
It’s easy to find people who see the world as zero-sum, and want to kill the
dreams of others in the hope of furthering their own ambitions.  One way you
fight that is to dream big and act accordingly, and to support those around you
who are doing the same.
